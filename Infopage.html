Waterfalls You Should Visit

1. Elephant Falls, Meghalaya

Elephant Falls is located in Shillong, Meghalaya. It's a stunning cascade with three tiers of falls.

2. Dudhsagar Waterfalls, Goa

Dudhsagar Waterfalls is one of the tallest waterfalls in India, located on the Mandovi River in Goa.

3. Athirappilly Waterfalls, Kerala

Athirappilly Waterfalls is the largest waterfall in Kerala, located in the Thrissur district. It is often referred to as the "Niagara of India".

4. Bhagsu Waterfalls, Himachal Pradesh

Bhagsu Waterfalls is located in McLeod Ganj, Himachal Pradesh. It's a serene and beautiful waterfall near the Bhagsunath Temple.

5. Soochippara Falls, Kerala

Soochippara Falls, also known as Sentinel Rock Waterfalls, is located in Wayanad, Kerala. It is surrounded by lush green forests.

6. Jog Falls, Karnataka

Jog Falls, created by the Sharavathi River, is the second highest plunge waterfall in India, located in Karnataka.

7. Nohkalikai Falls, Meghalaya

Nohkalikai Falls is the tallest plunge waterfall in India, located near Cherrapunji in Meghalaya.

8. Kempty Falls, Uttarakhand

Kempty Falls is a beautiful waterfall located in Mussoorie, Uttarakhand, and is a popular tourist attraction.

9. Shivanasamudra Falls, Karnataka

Shivanasamudra Falls is a segmented waterfall located in the Mandya district of Karnataka, formed by the Kaveri River.

10. Hogenakkal Falls, Tamil Nadu

Hogenakkal Falls, located on the Kaveri River in Tamil Nadu, is known for its unique rocky landscape and medicinal baths.

11. Meenmutty Falls, Kerala

Meenmutty Falls is a stunning three-tiered waterfall located in Wayanad, Kerala. It's one of the largest waterfalls in the region.
